On 4th September 1960, the club lifted its first Intercontinental Cup

0n 4 September 1960, Real Madrid secured the first of the club's six world champion titles. This victory came against Montevideo-based Peñarol in the old Intercontinental Cup, which was contested between the European Cup and Copa Libertadores holders. The Whites saw off the Uruguayan outfit in a two-legged final, the first leg of which took place on 3 July 1960 at Peñarol's ground and ended in a goalless stalemate.

A new master's degree at the Real Madrid Graduate School-Universidad Europea

The Master in Communication and Sports Journalism teaches what sports journalism is about and how it works, by analysing how the industry’s professional and social dimension has evolved nationally and internationally, including its legal, managing, marketing and economic aspects. The Master program trains students in journalistic work covering areas such as: Print journalism, television and sport, sports radio, digital journalism and social networks, and finally communication management in sports organizations from a cross-platform perspective.
